Universally Unique Identifier (UUID)

Universally Unique Identifier (UUID) is a 128 bit length hexadecimal string used as unique identifier (ID) in digital world.

It is not centralized (means no organization or authority is ensuring the uniqueness), but still it is unique.

Inside Microsoft ecosystem, it is called as Globally Unique Identifier (GUID).
Initially created by Apollo Computer
Later maintaining by Open Software Foundation (OSF) - aka Open Group
Recommended version UUID 5 (SHA-1) by RFC 4122
Example b689cf7c-c93a-5154-ace0-daa23ad0abf0
